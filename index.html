Sin Taxes

  Sin Taxes are a type of tax aimed it products deemed to be immoral or counter to the public good such as cigarettes, sugary drinks, or candy. The main argument for Sin Taxes is that they will reduce the consumption of these harmful products. The idea is that if something costs more people will buy less of it. One argument against this is that demand for addictive products may be relatively inelastic. (e.g. People who are addicted to cigarettes may buy them no matter how much they cost.)
